== Risk Assessment Matrix Prompt

=== Purpose

Risk assessment is crucial for successful software architecture projects. This prompt helps systematically identify, analyze, and plan mitigation strategies for technical, business, and operational risks that could impact your project.

=== Prompt Template

[source,text]
----
Help me create a comprehensive risk assessment matrix for my software architecture project. We'll systematically identify risks, assess their impact and probability, and develop mitigation strategies.

# Step 1: Project Context
First, tell me about your project context:
- Project scope and timeline
- Technology stack and constraints
- Team experience and size
- Business criticality
- Regulatory requirements
- Integration complexity

# Step 2: Risk Categories
Let's identify risks across different categories:

**Technical Risks**:
- Technology choices (unproven, obsolete, complex)
- Performance and scalability concerns
- Security vulnerabilities
- Integration challenges
- Data migration issues
- Technical debt accumulation

**Business Risks**:
- Changing requirements
- Budget constraints
- Market competition
- Regulatory changes
- Stakeholder conflicts
- Business model evolution

**Operational Risks**:
- Team capacity and skills
- Third-party dependencies
- Infrastructure limitations
- Deployment complexities
- Maintenance challenges
- Knowledge transfer issues

**External Risks**:
- Vendor lock-in
- Supply chain disruptions
- Economic factors
- Natural disasters
- Cyber security threats
- Legal/compliance issues

# Step 3: Risk Assessment Framework
For each risk, we'll assess:

**Probability Scale (1-5)**:
1. Very Low (0-10% chance)
2. Low (10-30% chance)
3. Medium (30-50% chance)
4. High (50-80% chance)
5. Very High (80-100% chance)

**Impact Scale (1-5)**:
1. Minimal (minor delays, minimal cost)
2. Low (< 1 month delay, < 10% cost increase)
3. Medium (1-3 months delay, 10-25% cost increase)
4. High (3-6 months delay, 25-50% cost increase)
5. Critical (> 6 months delay, > 50% cost increase, project failure)

**Risk Score = Probability × Impact**

**Risk Levels**:
- Low Risk: Score 1-6 (Monitor)
- Medium Risk: Score 8-12 (Plan mitigation)
- High Risk: Score 15-20 (Immediate action)
- Critical Risk: Score 25 (Emergency response)

# Step 4: Mitigation Strategies
For each risk, we'll define:

**Prevention**: Actions to reduce probability
**Mitigation**: Actions to reduce impact if risk occurs
**Contingency**: Backup plans if mitigation fails
**Transfer**: Insurance, contracts, outsourcing
**Acceptance**: Acknowledge and monitor

==== RISK-001: Database Technology Obsolescence
**Category**: Technical +
**Status**: Identified +
**Risk Level**: Medium (Score: 8) +

**Description**: 
The chosen database technology may become obsolete or lose vendor support during the project lifecycle, requiring costly migration and redevelopment.

**Probability Assessment**: Low (2/5)
- Technology has strong vendor backing
- Large community and ecosystem
- Recent major version releases

**Impact Assessment**: High (4/5)
- Would require complete data migration
- Significant code changes needed
- Potential service interruption
- Estimated 3-4 months additional development

**Mitigation Strategy**:
*Prevention*:
- Choose database with strong long-term support
- Implement database abstraction layer
- Regular technology roadmap reviews

*Mitigation*:
- Design for database portability
- Maintain migration scripts
- Regular backup and export procedures

*Contingency*:
- Alternative database evaluation completed
- Migration timeline and cost estimates prepared
- Vendor escalation contacts established

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Implement database abstraction layer (Due: [Date])
- [ ] Create database migration playbook (Due: [Date])
- [ ] Quarterly vendor roadmap review (Recurring)

**Review History**:

- [Date]: Mitigation strategy approved
- [Date]: Database abstraction layer implemented

**Category**: Operational +
**Status**: Active monitoring +
**Risk Level**: Medium (Score: 12) +

**Description**:
Critical team members with unique knowledge or skills leave the project, causing knowledge loss and delivery delays.

**Probability Assessment**: Medium (3/5)
- Competitive job market
- High-demand skills
- Project duration > 18 months

**Impact Assessment**: High (4/5)
- Knowledge transfer time required
- Temporary productivity loss
- Potential quality issues
- Recruitment and training costs

**Mitigation Strategy**:
*Prevention*:
- Knowledge sharing sessions
- Comprehensive documentation
- Cross-training initiatives
- Competitive retention packages

*Mitigation*:
- Rapid knowledge transfer protocols
- External consultant relationships
- Overlapping skill development

*Contingency*:
- Pre-qualified contractor pool
- Emergency knowledge extraction procedures
- Project scope reduction options

**Action Items**:
- [ ] Monthly knowledge sharing sessions (Recurring)
- [ ] Document critical architectural decisions (Ongoing)
- [ ] Cross-train team members on core components (Due: [Date])

**Status**: Active mitigation +

# Step 6: Risk Monitoring and Review
Establish regular risk review processes:

**Weekly**: High and critical risk status updates
**Monthly**: Complete risk register review
**Quarterly**: Risk landscape reassessment
**Major milestones**: Risk impact on timeline and scope

# Step 7: Risk Communication
Create communication strategies for:
- Stakeholder risk reporting
- Team risk awareness
- Escalation procedures
- Risk status dashboards

Let's start by discussing your project context and identifying the most significant risk categories for your system.
----

=== Usage Instructions

1. **Regular Reviews**: Assess risks continuously throughout the project
2. **Team Involvement**: Include diverse perspectives in risk identification
3. **Actionable Plans**: Ensure mitigation strategies are specific and measurable
4. **Monitor Trends**: Track how risks evolve over time
5. **Learn from Experience**: Update risk assessments based on lessons learned

=== Expected Output

The prompt generates:

* Comprehensive risk register
* Prioritized risk matrix
* Detailed mitigation strategies
* Action plans with ownership
* Regular review schedules

=== Integration with Architecture Documentation

Risk assessment should:

* Inform architecture decisions (ADRs)
* Connect to quality scenarios
* Guide technology choices
* Support project planning
